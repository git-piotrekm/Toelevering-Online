Quick Scan —
before we propose anything.

We sit down with UX/UI designers and Product Owners. No audit, no pitch. We want to see how the team works today — and whether we can help achieve better results in the UX/UI area.

Who
UX/UI designers + Product Owners
Both perspectives in one conversation. Design and product clarity together — so we don't miss the gap between intent and execution.
What it is
A conversation, not an assessment
Both sides leave with a clear picture of the situation. If we don't see potential — we say so.
What comes next
Only if we see something interesting
If we spot real opportunities, we propose AI coaching on the job. The discovery shapes the offer — not the other way around.

What Changes
Repetitive tasks go to AI — sketching variants, components, handoff descriptions. Designers get their time back.
Less operational work — space opens up for UX research and CRO initiatives that actually move the product.
Lasting habit change — after 6 months the team works differently, not because they had to, but because they saw it works.
On the job — we work on real projects together. AI isn't the training topic. It becomes the tool in the work.
