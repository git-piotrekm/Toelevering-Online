How we build QA 5 stages · measurement before and after

We build the QA process in five stages — with measurement from day one.

Before we change anything, we measure 2–3 key KPIs of the current process. These become the baseline: after implementation we show the difference in numbers, not in statements. QA is not an external audit — it is a knowledge transfer and the permanent embedding of quality into the team.

01 · DISCOVERY

Baseline

Assessment of process, scope and complexity. Measurement of 2–3 KPIs of the current state and scaling risks.

02 · STRATEGY

Test strategy

Risk assessment and identification of critical paths to ensure adequate test coverage. A clear division of roles and responsibilities for quality between QA, Development and Business. Collaboration and regular communication with the team and stakeholders at every stage of product development. Definition of the automation strategy and the scope of automated and manual tests for effective quality assurance.

03 · IMPLEMENTATION

Process in the team

Collaboration with development teams to effectively implement the QA strategy. Regular retrospectives and results analysis to adapt the approach to team needs. Continuous feedback loop supporting the development of processes and quality.

04 · AUTOMATYZACJA AI

Automation in the pipeline

Regression in CI/CD generated and maintained with Playwright + Cursor. More coverage, less manual work.

05 · MEASUREMENT

KPI after implementation

Re-measurement of the same KPIs. Before / after comparison and continuous improvement based on data.

QA Lead enters here

Starts alone: measures the baseline, designs the strategy and determines how many QA engineers are needed and for what.

additional QA join here

Testing, requirements completeness and change support through to release, then regression automation in CI.

Together with the team we work to define the most important KPIs that will show whether we are moving in the right direction as changes are implemented. The indicators below are an example starting point — measured before and after.

AI in automation
02 / 04
Playwright + Cursor stage 04 of the process · AI-assisted automation

Adopting Playwright — tests are written faster and maintenance costs less.

We combine Playwright (fast, stable E2E tests) with Cursor (AI in the editor). From acceptance criteria in a User Story, AI generates a test skeleton that QA verifies and finalises. The result: regression coverage grows faster, and the QA engineer stops writing repetitive code by hand and focuses on strategy and edge cases.

01 · INPUT

User story → test

Once connected to the repository (e.g. Azure DevOps) the acceptance criteria from a User Story become the starting point for AI.

02 · GENERATION

Cursor writes the skeleton

AI in Cursor creates tests and assertions aligned with the repo standard agreed with developers — in minutes, not hours.

03 · VERIFICATION

QA finalises

The engineer checks the logic, adds edge cases and stabilises flaky tests — quality remains on the human side.

04 · CI/CD

Regression in the pipeline

Regression tests run on every build. More frequent runs = faster bug detection.

Case study
03 / 04
Leadingcourses golf booking platform · 500k+ users / month

High traffic, zero regression — until QA entered from discovery.

Europe's largest multilingual golf course review and booking platform: 25,000 venues, a complex rating algorithm, expansion into new markets. Every production bug hits bookings directly. There was no coherent test strategy or automated regression — releases went out on the happy path and problems surfaced days later.

PRODUCT OWNER TIME SAVED
+ 1–2 dni / tydz.

The PO no longer manually tests high-risk changes — time returns to product work and quality improves.

REGRESSION COVERAGE (AUTOMATED)
0% → 40%

From zero automated tests to regression running in CI on every build — for critical booking paths.

HOTFIXES PER MONTH
4 → 1

Fewer urgent priority-1 fixes — the team sticks to the plan instead of fighting fires.

WHAT WE DID

QA Lead engaged from the discovery stage — responsible for assessing the current quality level, preparing the test strategy and the approach to regression automation in Playwright. Subsequently 2 QA engineers joined the team to support testing and requirements completeness verification. As a result, quality was built continuously throughout the entire development process, not only at the final testing stage.

Case study

ChannelEngine e-commerce integration platform · marketplaces · multiple clients

Fast releases without risk — thanks to regression in CI/CD.

ChannelEngine is a platform connecting sellers with dozens of marketplaces worldwide. Complex integration logic, multiple clients and frequent releases meant every production bug hit sales directly. There was no systematic regression or clear test strategy — changes were verified manually, which slowed the team down and created risk.

CRITICAL DEFECTS / MONTH
12 → 2

−83% incidents affecting client data during a high-risk refactor.

REGRESSION COVERAGE (AUTOMATED)
15% → 45%

Automated tests protecting features for 30 clients on every change during the React/SPA rewrite.

REGRESSION TESTS BEFORE RELEASE
3 dni → ½ dnia

Shorter and more reliable release cycle — pipelines took over regression instead of people.

WHAT WE DID

QA Lead engaged from the start: assessing the current quality state, developing the test strategy and launching automated regression in Playwright within the CI/CD pipelines. As a result every release is verified automatically, and the team can deliver changes faster and with greater confidence in their quality.
