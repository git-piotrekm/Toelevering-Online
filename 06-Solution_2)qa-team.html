How we build QA 5 stages · measurement from day one

We build QA in five stages — with measurement from day one.

Before we change anything, we measure 2–3 key KPIs of the current process. These are the baseline: after implementation we show the difference in numbers, not statements. Right now bugs reach production after a few days, sprints break, and when the configurator connects to shop, logistics and PIM — one failure means a customer can't place an order. That changes.

01 · DISCOVERY

Baseline

Process assessment, scope and complexity review. Measure 2–3 KPIs of the current state and scaling risks.

02 · STRATEGY

Test strategy

Test pyramid and quadrants, branch and environment strategy. Focus on configurator ↔ shop ↔ logistics ↔ PIM integration.

03 · IMPLEMENTATION

Process in team

Early exploratory tests, QA in refinement, acceptance criteria and testable stories from the start — not just happy path.

04 · AI AUTOMATION

Safety net

Regression in CI/CD generated and maintained with Playwright + Cursor. More coverage, less manual work.

05 · MEASUREMENT

KPIs after

Re-measure the same KPIs. Before / after comparison and continuous improvement on data.

QA Lead enters here

One person starts the process: measures baseline and designs the strategy.

+2 QA join here

Daily testing, requirements completeness, release support.

+1 QA Automation

Dedicated to Playwright automations and regression in CI/CD on Azure.

We measure before & after — 3 KPIs · Bugs on production (escape rate) · Regression coverage (% automated) · Sprints broken by hotfixes (rework)
AI automation
02 / 04
Playwright + Cursor stage 04 of the process · AI-assisted automation

Tests are written faster, maintenance costs less.

We combine Playwright (fast, stable E2E tests) with Cursor (AI in the editor). From acceptance criteria and recorded user flows AI generates a test skeleton, and QA verifies and hardens it. Result: regression coverage grows faster, and the QA engineer stops writing boilerplate and focuses on strategy and edge cases.

01 · INPUT

User story → test

Acceptance criteria and a recorded flow in Playwright Codegen become the input material for AI.

02 · GENERATION

Cursor writes scaffold

AI in Cursor creates tests, assertions and Page Objects aligned with repo standards — in minutes, not hours.

03 · VERIFICATION

QA hardens it

Engineer reviews logic, adds edge cases and tames flaky tests — quality judgement stays with humans.

04 · CI/CD

Regression in pipeline

The suite grows and runs on every build on Azure. Cursor also helps maintain and fix broken selectors.

Case study
03 / 04
Leadingcourses golf booking platform · 500k+ users / month · e-commerce

High traffic, zero regressions — once QA entered from discovery.

Europe's largest multilingual golf booking platform: 25,000 venues, a complex scoring algorithm, expansion to new markets. Every production bug hits bookings. No consistent testing strategy, no automated regression — releases went out on the happy path and problems surfaced days later. Sound familiar?

PRODUCTION BUGS / MONTH
18 → 4

−78% bugs reaching the user after deploying automated and integration tests.

REGRESSION COVERAGE (AUTOMATED)
0% → 65%

Regression in CI running on every build — a safety net for critical booking paths.

SPRINTS BROKEN BY HOTFIXES
40% → 8%

Fewer sprint interruptions for firefighting — the team returns to the roadmap.

WHAT WE DID

QA Lead from discovery: baseline measurement, test strategy and quadrants, Playwright regression automation (Cursor-assisted) in CI/CD pipelines. Then 2 QA Engineers for daily testing and requirements completeness, plus 1 QA for automation — quality built into the team, not glued on at the end.

Case study
04 / 04
Polavis patient portals for hospitals · Berlin · ISO 27001

Full React/SPA rewrite — zero regressions for 30 clients.

Berlin-based e-health platform: patient portals integrated with hospital systems, compliant with KHZG and ISO 27001, handling sensitive medical data. Full rebuild to React/SPA while maintaining complete functionality for 30 existing clients. Without a regression net, every change risked a production failure at a real hospital.

CRITICAL BUGS / MONTH
12 → 2

−83% incidents touching patient data during a high-risk refactor.

REGRESSION COVERAGE (AUTOMATED)
15% → 80%

Automated tests protecting 30-client functionality on every commit during the React migration.

MANUAL REGRESSION BEFORE RELEASE
3 days → ½ day

Shorter, safer release cycle — regression handed off to pipelines instead of people.

WHAT WE DID

QA as safety net during refactor: baseline at start, regression strategy for critical clinical paths, Playwright automations in CI/CD wired to every build. QA in requirements and acceptance criteria ensured that no feature used by hospitals disappeared after migration to React/SPA.
